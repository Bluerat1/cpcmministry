Our Story

Reaching the Forgotten with God's Grace

Christian Partnership for the Confined Ministry (CPCM) was born out of a burning conviction that the Gospel is for everyone — including those the world has forgotten behind prison walls. What began as small, faith-filled visits to a local jail grew into a full-scale ministry touching thousands of lives across multiple correctional facilities in the Philippines.

Fueled by love, driven by Scripture, and sustained by the faithfulness of God, CPCM has become a beacon of hope for Persons Deprived of Liberty (PDLs) and their families.

Our Vision

Every person behind bars encountering the living God — becoming a transformed disciple who impacts communities.

Our Mission

To evangelize, disciple, and equip prisoners and ex-PDLs through biblical training and holistic programs.

Our Ministries

A holistic approach — reaching people inside prison walls and walking with them all the way to restoration.

📣

Evangelism

Regular Gospel proclamation inside jail facilities, reaching PDLs with the message of salvation.

📚

Discipleship

Systematic Bible study and spiritual formation for those who have accepted Christ.

🎓

Biblical & Leadership Training

In-depth pastoral classes equipping PDLs to lead fellowships and plant churches.

Continued training and mentorship for ex-PDLs after their release.

✝️

Pastoral / Leadership Class

Ongoing theological education for ex-PDLs called to ministry and church planting.

🌾

Livelihood Program

Practical skills training and support to help ex-PDLs rebuild their lives.

👧

Children of Prisoners

Scholarship program and spiritual care for the children of those behind bars.

Halfway House

Rebels Returnee Ministry

Outreach for former rebels and surrenderees through reintegration and spiritual transformation.

Values Education

Drug Awareness Symposiums

Police Ministry

Spiritual care, chaplaincy, and Bible study for law enforcement officers.

Our Heart

Why Share God's Love Behind Bars?

Prisoners are among the most forgotten people in society. Yet Jesus made it clear — visiting those in prison is an act of love done unto Him.

📖

God's Command

Scripture calls us to remember those in prison as though we ourselves were suffering with them (Hebrews 13:3).

♟️

Strategic Impact

Transformed prisoners go back to their barrios and become some of the most powerful witnesses for Christ in the most difficult places.

🕊️

No Life is Beyond Grace

The grace of God knows no bars. Every person in a cell is someone Jesus died for.
